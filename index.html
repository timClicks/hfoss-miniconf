Details

What it is

The focus of the event is to enable contributors to free software projects in humaniatarian events to share experiences. Topic areas are likely to diverge from software to a degree and touch upon cultural challenges dealing with emergency management, how to keep a deployment running for 24h and so forth.

What to expect

The event aims to be highly participatory and interactive. There are likely to be unconference elements to the day, to facilitate knowledge sharing.

Participants will come from a large range, with a common interest in supporting communities come through adverse events. This means that you should expect to have your status quo challenged. from emergency managers, people interested in helping with projects

Related Projects

Ushahidi

Ushahidi is a mapping tool designed for displaying infomation from the crowd. It has been extremely successful at distributing information widely in an very fast turn-around.

Sahana Eden

The Sahana Eden platform allows for the development of applications for disaster management, international development and environmental management.

Humanitarian OpenStreetMap Team (HOT)

HOT provides mapping support in the event of emergencies. The team also works with vulnerable communities to improve their preparation for disasters.
